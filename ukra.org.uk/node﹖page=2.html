The United Kingdom Rocketry Association (UKRA) promotes and represents high power, medium power and model rocketry in the United Kingdom for amateur research, educational and recreational purposes. UKRA is also the specialist technical body to the BMFA with responsibilities for High Power Rocketry.

New safety code and upcoming AGM

Submitted by Stuart on Wed, 05/08/2020 - 07:07

UKRA is delighted to announce the release of version 7.0 of the UKRA Safety Code. This version includes a significant overhaul in document layout, improved clarification of key guidance and updated distance tables to reflect modern safety considerations. The new version of this safety code is available on the dedicated Safety Code page here.

New UK Altitude record

Submitted by Andy on Wed, 26/06/2019 - 20:42

Team Sunr1de at the University of Sheffield successfully set a new UK national altitude record at Spaceport America, New Mexico, USA on Friday 21st June 2019.

The rocket 'Helen' reached an altitude of 36,274 ft on a CTI N5800 C-Star motor and was successfully recovered

UKRA Council Resignation

Submitted by Stuart on Sun, 05/05/2019 - 02:56

UKRA regrets to announce the resignation of Charles Simpson from the UKRA Council. Charles is resigning to pursue other interests, including his own rocketry.

Charles has been an enormous contributor to rocketry for decades and has worked tirelessly behind the scenes to improve the hobby for all members of the community, especially with regard to how rocketry is viewed by government and the authorities.
